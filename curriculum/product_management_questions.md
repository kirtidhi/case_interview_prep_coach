# Product Management Question Bank

This document contains a curated list of generalized, highly effective interview questions designed to test various competencies within Product Management. These questions have been scrubbed of company-specific references to provide a universal coaching resource.

---

## 1. Product & Business Insights (Case Questions)
*Test the user's ability to think critically, analyze markets, and solve complex product problems.*

* **Headquarters Relocation:** Imagine your team is interested in moving its main headquarters. The team believes this will yield cost savings and increase productivity. How would you find and recommend a suitable location? What factors do you consider? What obstacles might you face, and what data would you use to influence leadership?
* **Platform Conversion Promo:** We are running a promo to convert desktop users to mobile app users. What should you measure? How do you design an experiment? How do you measure potential harm, and how do you determine the right frequency cap for the promo?
* **Product Sunset:** A VP of Product is re-evaluating the portfolio and asks you to help think through whether to sunset a particular product. How would you approach this? What are the key considerations and who would you involve?
* **Competitive Threat:** Your company's enterprise software suite is being challenged by a smaller, more focused player winning market share with a lower-cost point solution. Senior management is worried. Walk through appropriate countermeasures and their short/long-term trade-offs.
* **Churn Algorithm:** Given a dataset of credit card users, design an algorithm to determine whether a user is new, a consistent spender, or churned. How do you handle edge cases (e.g., someone who only buys ski tickets once a year)?
* **Retail Expansion:** How would you determine if a major tech company should build more physical brick-and-mortar stores to sell its hardware devices?
* **New Audience Launch:** A tech company makes smartwatches and fitness trackers for adults under two distinct brand names. They are considering a new wearable aimed at kids. What do you need to know to recommend launching or not? If launched, which brand should be used?
* **M&A Market Sizing:** Your company wants to acquire a BI/data visualization startup to combine with its data warehouse. How would you estimate the size of the BI market and the revenue potential of the acquisition?
* **Internal Program Design:** Your team wants to create a mentorship program for new hires to increase productivity and retention. What are the key components? What data would be useful to design it, and how would you measure impact?
* **Stock Analysis:** Imagine a major video platform has its own stock. As an analyst, give it a Buy, Sell, or Hold rating. Walk through the parameters you'd consider and the associated risks.
* **Internal IT Strategy:** You work in strategy for an internal IT division, managing the mix of products offered to employees. How do you evaluate what "good" looks like and make decisions?
* **Internationalization Strategy:** Your company acquired a popular video platform only available in the US. You are tasked with its worldwide launch strategy. How do you determine which countries to launch in first?
* **Moonshot GTM:** Your company has developed a stylish hat that can read brain waves. How would you develop the business case and take this product to market?
* **Mobile Strategy:** Imagine you are driving strategy for a major email platform to increase mobile app usage. What factors do you consider and who do you collaborate with?
* **Crisis/Legal Management:** A week before launching a marketing campaign for a new toy, Legal informs you that two regions passed new age-based testing laws and recommends a hold. How do you research the impact and decide how to proceed?
* **Regional Pricing Trade-offs:** You work for a global cloud storage subscription service with consistent pricing ($1.99/mo). A regional VP proposes cutting the price to $0.99/mo for their specific emerging market. How do you respond, and what are the trade-offs of global vs. market-specific pricing?
* **Hardware ROI:** A product lead wants to know if a new hardware product is a good business investment. The product has negative margins offset by lifetime value (LTV). How do you determine the right price? If revenue drops in Q1 post-launch, how do you analyze the root cause?
* **Hardware vs. Software:** Why would a primarily software-focused company invest in building first-party hardware rather than just partnering with OEMs?

## 2. Business Acumen & Operations (Case Questions)
*Test the user's ability to scale processes, measure success, and align cross-functional teams.*

* **Org Design (OKRs):** How would you design the annual OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) process for a brand new organization from scratch?
* **Reconciling Data:** You are coordinating business reviews. One cross-functional group shows great progress tracking their way, while another group shows the opposite using a different method. How do you reconcile these differences and communicate it to management?
* **Success Metrics for AI:** Imagine you are tasked with establishing success metrics for a new generative AI product. What are the top 3 most important metrics and why?
* **Herding Cats:** You need data from different teams for an urgent strategy presentation next week. The deadline is approaching, and a couple of teams haven't responded to your requests for key data points. How do you approach this?
* **Hard-to-Quantify Goals:** What are examples of hard-to-quantify engineering objectives? How do you set goals and measure progress for them?
* **Executive Dashboard:** Pick your favorite tech product. What metrics would you put on the executive dashboard?
* **Disparate Dashboards:** A VP wants a unified executive dashboard, but currently, everyone uses different, disparate dashboards. How do you deliver on this when data might be inaccessible and teams are resistant?
* **Revamping Cadences:** The GM wants to revamp operational cadences and reporting. You need to gather feedback from their direct reports (VPs/Directors). How do you approach this, and how do you handle a VP who is vehemently opposed to your recommendations?
* **Shifting Priorities:** You are driving a change of priorities in a very successful organization. What is the challenge with that, and where do you start?
* **Market Prioritization:** Given a dataset of 5 global markets with varying user bases, buying users, revenue, and Y/Y growth, which market is the top growth driver and how do you prioritize resourcing?
* **Vertical Investment:** You need to estimate the benefits of a search engine increasing its investment in a specific vertical (e.g., Video Games). What are the benefits and how do you quantify them?
* **The Next Big Thing:** Imagine you are tasked with deciding what a tech giant's next big investment should be. How do you approach this task and what is your recommendation?
* **Enterprise Expansion:** A Business Development lead says there's an opportunity to help a consumer mobile OS grow in the enterprise space (e.g., on non-smartphones). How do you vet this and structure a recommendation?
* **Wearables Value:** Why build a wearables operating system? What is the monetization opportunity, and where is the category headed?
* **Carrier Partnerships:** A mobile OS team is evaluating whether to work with a major international carrier to launch a modified, region-specific version of the OS. What information do you need to advise on this strategy?
* **Cost Reduction:** A sales team is facing economic pressure. You are asked to create a plan to reduce sales costs *without* decreasing revenue or headcount. How do you create efficiencies?
* **Global Hardware Expansion:** Choose a hardware product available in limited markets and walk through a strategy to expand it globally.
* **B2B Distributor Partnerships:** You are exploring partnerships with major home security distributors (e.g., ADT) to sell your company's smart home devices. They want access to user data, remote control, and unique SKUs. How do you approach this partnership?
* **Annual Planning:** You are leading annual planning for a major product area. Execs ask you to prioritize all initiatives (product and program). How do you think about prioritization?
* **Strategy from Scratch:** You join an engineering org operating without a strategy, facing shifting user demands. How do you formulate a strategy to align disparate functions?
* **Competitor Outperformance:** A VP asks why a specific competitor is outperforming your company in the wearables space. What metrics/data do you look at, and how do you fix it?
* **Unexpected Reorg:** You've spent months preparing an organizational transformation. Two weeks before launch, you learn a major leadership change is coming, and the org might not exist in a month. What do you do?
* **Emerging Tech Strategy:** How would you determine the right Augmented Reality (AR) or Virtual Reality (VR) strategy for a tech giant?

## 3. Behavioral & Leadership
*Test the user's emotional intelligence, resilience, and collaborative skills using the S.T.A.R. method.*

* **Impact & Innovation:** Provide two examples of end-to-end initiatives you led that were your highest accomplishments—one for innovation/creativity, and one for business impact.
* **Non-Standard Solution:** Tell me about a time you brought a "non-standard" or controversial solution to the table. How did you convince your stakeholders?
* **Simplification vs. Robustness:** Tell me about a time you drastically simplified operations. Alternatively, a time you created more robust/detailed operations.
* **Process Inefficiency:** Tell me about a time you proactively identified a clear process inefficiency and fixed it.
* **Data vs. Intuition:** Can you share an experience where data showed a different insight than your intuition? How did you proceed and convince stakeholders?
* **Ethical Pressure:** Tell me about a time you felt significant pressure to break the rules or compromise your values to accomplish a goal.
* **Inclusive Design:** Tell me about a time a product worked well for some users, but not for others. How did you identify and fix the issue?
* **Regret:** Tell me about a decision you made in the last year or two that you would change if you could.
* **Fostering Inclusion:** Can you tell me about a time when you helped foster a more accepting or inclusive work culture?
* **Diverse Perspectives:** Tell me about a time a stronger decision was reached because different viewpoints were leveraged.
* **Tight Deadlines & Pushback:** Four weeks before a major launch event, a senior executive asks for an additional feature. Your engineers are completely maxed out. How do you handle this?
* **Change Management:** Imagine you are leading an org with a low tolerance for change, and several major changes are coming. How do you lead through this?
* **Siloed Teams:** After working on a project, you discover another team worked on the exact same problem and no information was shared. How do you resolve this?
* **Late-Stage Bugs:** At the end of a testing sprint, you notice issues for certain user subgroups, but you have tight time constraints before the next sprint. What do you do?
* **Rogue Contributors:** A person outside your project volunteers code for a feature they need, but it ignores the broader picture and creates challenges. They aren't receptive to feedback. How do you handle it?
* **Remote Collaboration:** Imagine working jointly on a project remotely with someone you’ve never met. How do you establish a good working relationship?
* **Unpopular Decisions:** You planned a team offsite based on a majority vote, but a subset of people tell you they do not want to attend the winning option. How do you respond?
* **Conflict Resolution:** Two teammates have differing opinions on a project, but decisions must be made immediately. How do you help them decide?
* **Changing Minds:** Tell me about a time you changed your opinion on a co-worker from negative to positive.
* **Failure:** Tell me about a time you set a difficult goal for yourself that you did not achieve.
* **First-Time Tasks:** Tell me about a task you recently did for the very first time. What was the biggest obstacle?
